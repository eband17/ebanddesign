HOME OFFICE

Hidden Home Office Style

Concealed within furniture or cabinetry to maintain a clean and uncluttered look in the living space. Desks and workstations can be hidden behind fold-out panels or doors.

Library Home Office Style

Library-style home offices are designed for book lovers and often feature floor-to-ceiling bookshelves, cozy reading nooks, and classic furniture like leather armchairs.

Gamer's Home Office Style

A gamer's home office is equipped with gaming consoles, computers, and comfortable gaming chairs. It may also feature gaming-themed decor and accessories.

Industrial Style

Feature exposed brick walls, metal accents, and concrete floors. Vintage accessories, such as Edison bulb lighting, add to the industrial look.

Scandinavian Style

Typically features light-colored furniture, minimalistic design, and an uncluttered workspace. Natural wood and white decor elements are common.

Minimalist Style

Minimalist home offices focus on simplicity, decluttering, and functionality. They often feature clean lines, neutral colors, and a reduction of unnecessary decor items for a sleek and uncluttered workspace.

Green and Sustainable Style

Features recycled or repurposed furniture, energy-efficient lighting, and indoor plants to promote a healthy and environmentally conscious workspace.

Traditional Style

Traditional home offices feature classic and timeless design elements, often with dark wood furniture, ornate details, and a formal ambiance. They typically incorporate classic bookshelves, leather seating, and antique-inspired decor.

Transitional Style

Blend elements of both traditional and modern styles. They aim for a harmonious balance between classic and contemporary design, offering versatility and timelessness.

Kids' Homework Station

A kids' homework station is designed to provide a dedicated workspace for children to complete their schoolwork. It often includes age-appropriate desks, chairs, and storage solutions.

Monochromatic Style

Monochromatic home offices focus on a single color scheme, creating a cohesive and visually pleasing workspace. This can make the room feel more organized and less distracting.

Home Office with a View

If your home has a scenic view, you can design your home office to maximize that view with large windows and strategically placed workspaces. This can provide inspiration and tranquility.
